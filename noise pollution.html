Noise Pollution

Noise pollution, also known as sound pollution is any kind of unwanted noise or an excessive amount of noise that affects human and animal health. Even though sound can seem like just a minor annoyance or a temporary disturbance, long exposure to sound such as from a construction site, airport or other loud environment can cause long term damages. Unfortunately, excessive noise is extremely common as show in Another study by the US Administration’s Traffic Noise Model found similar findings as 11.3% of daytime civilians encountered detrimental levels of noise and 3.7% at night in College Park, Georgia. Noise is constantly around us, but when it gets too loud and too frequent it becomes a threat to our health

To summarise the physical health risks, constant exposure to loud noise can create higher risk of cardiovascular diseases, coronary heart diseases, result in lost hearing, high blood pressure, trouble sleeping and heart arrhythmia. But noise carries with it issues to our mental state as well, commonly headaches and lack of focus, along with anxiety, stress, mental instability, fatigue and improper cognitive development in children, although these are more extreme. About 75-85dB is when sound is considered harmful, anything beforehand is safe even for long exposure (unless sleeping, in which case 45dB and above is harmful).

Generally places with heavy duty technology, explosions, transportation and many people are the loudest of environments. Another great way to gauge how loud an area is, is by seeing how urbanised it is because most of the loudest sounds are man-made, including people themselves. Some common environments that are loud are construction sites, airports, busy roads and crowds of people.


What can we do against this?

Luckily, noise pollution is one of the easiest pollutants to fix for yourself and your neighbours, some of the easiest small scale steps include:

  • Close your windows
  • Cover your ears if it’s an immediate, sudden noise
  • Put on earplugs
  • Back away further from the noise
  • Place your head into a noise absorbing object, like a pillow
  • Shut the door
  • Turn off or lower the volume of electronic devices
  • Counteract the unpleasant sound with pleasant sounds

However, if you live in a permanently noisy area, more long term solutions should be incorporated, these include:

  • Create awareness about noise pollution because most neglect it or understate it on account of sound being temporary and invisible
  • Have a more enclosed house design, with more walls separating each room
  • Get acoustics on the walls to absorb and contain sound, carpets have the same effect but for the floor
  • Get curtains
  • Muffle loud sounds on sound source itself
  • And of course don’t create loud noises yourself, for the sake of your health and those around you
